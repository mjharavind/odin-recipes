Karuvaadu Kulambu

Description

Karuvaadu Kulambu (Dry Fish Curry) is a popular South Indian dish, especially in Tamil Nadu. It is made with sun-dried fish (karuvaadu) cooked in a tangy tamarind-based curry with spices. The dry fish adds a unique flavor and aroma to the dish.

This curry is usually served with steamed rice and is loved for its bold and traditional taste. It is a comforting dish that remind many of home-cooked meals and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 3 tsp coriander seeds
  • 6-7 dry red chillies
  • 1 tsp cumin sedds (jeera)
  • A few curry leaves
  • Tamarind (for tamarind water)
  • Oil (for frying)
  • 1 tsp mustard sedds (kadugu)
  • Dry fish (karuvaadu)
  • 1 brinjal (kathirikai), chopped
  • Small pieces of coconut
  • 4-5 roasted chana dal (pottu kadala)
  • 1 tsp fennel sedds (sombu)

Steps

  1. Dry roast coriander seeds, dry red chillies, cumin seeds,and curry leaves. Grind them into a fine powder.
  2. Prepare tamarind water and mix it with the ground spice powder.
  3. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and curry leaves. Once they splutter, add dried fish (karuvaadu) and fry until aromatic
  4. Add chopped brinjal (eggplant) and pour in the prepared tamarind water.
  5. Cook until the mixture starts boiling and is 75% done.
  6. Add small coconut pieces, roasted chana dal, and ground fennel seeds. Let the gravy cook further until fully done.
